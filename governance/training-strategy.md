# Security Awareness Training & Targeted Implementation Strategy

## 1. Business Vulnerability Assessment
A critical responsibility of the Security Awareness Team is determining which business units exhibit the highest vulnerability to social engineering. By analyzing simulated phishing click-rates and past incident logs, high-risk departments are isolated for targeted education.

## 2. Targeted Training Implementation Matrix


| Vulnerable Business Unit | Identified Risk Factor | Targeted Training Deployment | Success Metric |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Finance & Accounting** | Target for Business Email Compromise (BEC) and fake invoice scams. | Mandatory courses on wire-transfer verification protocols and dual-authorization procedures. | 0% compliance failures on external financial requests. |
| **Human Resources (HR)** | Vulnerable to malicious resume attachments and fake job inquiries. | Training on sandboxed attachment execution and macro-disabling security best practices. | 95% or higher reporting rate of suspicious external attachments. |
| **Sales & Marketing** | High volume of external communication makes them prone to credential harvesting links. | Short, interactive micro-learning modules focused on URL inspection and multi-factor authentication (MFA) prompts. | 50% reduction in phishing simulation click-rates within 90 days. |

## 3. Continuous Improvement Procedures
Security training is not a one-time event. The team implements a continuous feedback loop:
* **Post-Training Simulations:** Launching unannounced, simulated phishing campaigns 14 days after training to measure knowledge retention.
* **Remediation Pathways:** Users who fail a simulation are automatically enrolled in targeted, 5-minute refresher courses rather than punitive measures, fostering a positive security culture.
